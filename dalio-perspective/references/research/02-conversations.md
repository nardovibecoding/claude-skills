## 一、Charlie Rose 對話（2022年，Changing World Order 書籍宣傳）

**背景**：深度對話，約1小時，討論新書《Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order》，涵蓋宏觀經濟、美中關係、民主危機

### 論為何開始寫書
[一手] "Well, when I started the transition, I had all these principles that I wrote down, and they're like recipes. And I decided, well, I thought I'd pass them along."

[一手] "Because one thing I learned is that many times I was surprised in my life. It's because of things that never happened in my lifetime before, but happened many times in history. And so for that reason, I needed to study the 500 years."

[一手] "Before I was very, very, very private. And also I didn't have any desire to share. Now I have a desire really to pass things along."

### 論如何研究歷史
[一手] "I'd like to do it as though I'm going through the real experience. I like to not read history in retrospect, but read the papers as things are transpiring, and imagine that I'm going through it."

[一手] "I speak with people. I'm very lucky that I could speak with people like Henry Kissinger and others who they teach me about the history and the many dimensions... I speak to the best people, including the equivalents in China and others, to learn."

### 論歷史因果關係的永恆性
[一手] "The cause-effect relationships are by and large timeless. There is evolution... But because if they're put in the same position, let's say a Federal Reserve is put into a position of having to decide, do you have defaults or do you print money? There's a choice. And that choice repeats over history."

### 1971年震撼時刻（尼克松黃金脫鉤）
[一手] "1971, I was clerking on the floor of the stock exchange. And Nixon got on television on Sunday night, and I watched him tell the country, and tell the world, that the promise to take paper dollars and turn them in for gold was going to be defaulted on. And I was shocked. I went down on the floor of the exchange... I thought it would go down a lot. It went up a lot. I was wrong. So I studied history."

[一手] "The exact same thing happened on March 5, 1933 by Roosevelt, doing it over the radio, the exact same thing. And so that taught me that I needed to do that."

### 論通貨膨脹的本質
[一手] "There are two types of inflation. There's an inflation when the demand is pressing up against the ability to produce... There's also a monetary inflation. And a monetary inflation means, very simply, if you produce more money in credit, if that increase is greater than the production of the goods and services, prices will go up."

[一手] "I don't think monetary inflation is paid enough attention to."

### 論美元的脆弱性
[一手] "It's the only unique, really most important power of the United States... Think about it. You can print the money that the world will accept. Now, that means when they accept it, they buy your bonds."

[一手] "The weaponization of the dollar in the form of sanctions" — 指出美元霸權的第二大威脅

### 論中國的戰略意圖
[一手] "The main reason that China did not denominate its exports and trade in the currency... is that they didn't want to be a threat to the United States."

[一手] "I think that they would probably be just as happy if you divided the world in half. And they were on their half. And they had that sphere of influence. And we had our half. And there was peace in the world."

[一手] "An objective is not to dominate the world. An objective is to operate in a way where... they are as strong and powerful and healthy and prosperous as they can be."

### 論民主危機與內戰風險
[一手] "I put the odds of something like that at about 40 percent." （指美國出現「rule of law breaks down」的權力博弈情景）

[一手] "A civil war... that the rule of law and Constitution can be of diminished value relative to power, such as in the elections, neither side accepting losing, such as the Supreme Court makes rulings that are not followed by states..."

[一手] "When the causes that people are behind are more important to them than the system, the system is in jeopardy."

### 論教育與資本主義改革
[一手] "Parents who will take good care of you. A good public education system. Add equal opportunity. That's all you need. That's all it has existed throughout history."

[一手] "Every system needs to be improved and modified and made better, right? So, and I'm a mechanic. So I just look at the cause effect relationship."

[一手] "The profit system alone is not good enough." （談資本主義缺陷）

### 論如何解決（假設執政）
[一手] "What I'd like to do is put metrics of health. Those 18 metrics... I publish them so everyone could see those things. And you could, like a health check. It's like biofeedback."

[一手] "I'd have like the equivalent of a Manhattan Project, that they go, I almost don't care what the particular policies are. But if you have smart bipartisan policies that will improve productivity for the broad-based population..."

## 二、"Dark Times" 播客訪談（2024年前後）

**背景**：年輕創業者受眾，問及個人如何應對世界變局、如何在暗黑時代生存

### 論個人生命週期
[一手] "They have to understand how the life cycle works... You have to understand that you're on an adventurous journey, okay? And you have a certain nature... And then you're going after the things that you like, and you're learning, and it's going to have the ups and downs and so on."

[一手] "What's your nature? How's the journey? How do you find the path that's suitable to your nature? And then you'll go down that path, and you'll discover, and you'll evolve."

### 論創業者選擇地理位置
[一手] "The US has a culture of entrepreneurship inventiveness. There's a whole different culture, like in Europe, generally speaking... And here you could be 25, have a blue streak in your hair and have the talent. But if you got the talent to pull it off, you can get the resources and you can be an entrepreneur. There's a culture of that."

### 論美國的問題
[一手] "The bottom 60%, 60% of Americans have below a 6th grade reading level."

[一手] "About 3 million people in a country of 330 million people are really unbelievably doing great... At the same time, the bottom 60% — that population, in terms of the basics, being productive, you have to be productive and be prosperous."

### 論科技戰
[一手] "The winner of the technology war is going to win all wars. Okay. In other words, they'll win the economic war. They'll win the geopolitical war because technology."

### 論「人民總以為帝國不會衰落」
[一手] "People always think that the future would be a slightly modified version of the present. And it's not."

[一手] "These evolutions always take place... The United States is facing a number of those challenges and we're at a moment where it's a question of whether the system and the people can get control of the situations."

[二手，via CNBC headline] "Billionaire Ray Dalio: Going broke was one of the best things that happened to me" （Jun 2022）
