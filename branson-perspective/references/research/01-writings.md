# Richard Branson — First-Person Writings & Authored Sources

> Agent Research | Date: 2026-04-07

> **Research Note**: Branson is prolific in public writing — autobiography, blog, books, and interviews. Primary first-person sources: "Losing My Virginity" (1998), "Business Stripped Bare" (2008), "The Virgin Way" (2014), "Finding My Virginity" (2017/updated 2024), and an extensive personal blog on virgin.com. Unlike many business figures, his written voice is almost identical to his spoken voice.

---

## 一、Autobiography: "Losing My Virginity" (1998 / updated 2024)

**Source**: Virgin Books, 1998. Updated as "Losing and Finding My Virginity" (Portfolio/Penguin, 2024). The central first-person document of his life.

### Core thesis
[一手] "I have always lived my life by thriving on opportunity and adventure. Some of the best ideas come out of the blue, and you have to keep an open mind to see their virtue."

### On starting Virgin Records
[一手] "I was young, broke, but absolutely convinced that my instincts were right. I believed that if I could create a label where artists felt supported, we'd attract the best talent."

### On selling Virgin Records (1992) — most painful decision
[二手, widely quoted] He reportedly cried in the street after signing the sale to EMI for $1 billion, saying it felt like "selling a child." The sale was necessary to keep Virgin Atlantic alive during Gulf War fuel costs.
[推断] This moment is his clearest illustration of a core principle: the adventure (airline) took priority over nostalgia (the music he loved). Purpose over sentiment.

### On the Virgin Atlantic founding moment
[一手] "I was in Puerto Rico and I'd been bumped by American Airlines. I was determined to get to see my girlfriend in the Virgin Islands. I went to the back of the airport, hired a plane, wrote on a blackboard as a joke: 'Virgin Airlines, one way, $39.' I went around all the people who'd been bumped and filled up my first plane."

### On protecting the downside
[一手] "One of the most important phrases in my life is 'protect the downside.' I negotiated with Boeing so that if the airline didn't work in year one, they'd take the plane back."

## 二、"Business Stripped Bare" (2008)

**Source**: Virgin Books. His most focused business philosophy book — less autobiography, more operating manual.

### People first
[一手] "Train staff so they are good enough to leave, but treat them so well they don't want to."
[一手] "The key is to hire for personality and values, and then train for skills. Most companies do it the other way around."

### On delegation
[一手] "Virgin could never have evolved from a student magazine to a global brand if I hadn't learned how to trust people, collaborate, and delegate."

### On entering industries
[一手] "We only go into businesses where the customer has been ripped off or underserved, where confusion reigns, and the competition is complacent."

## 三、"The Virgin Way" (2014)

**Source**: Portfolio/Penguin. His leadership philosophy — listening, learning, laughing, leading.

### On listening as competitive advantage
[一手] "I have always believed that listening more than I speak has helped me come up with all kinds of ideas. I carry a notebook everywhere and write down everything I observe."

### On fun as a first principle
[一手] "When you're passionate about what you're doing then you'll not only do it better but you'll be far more successful at it. If it isn't fun, something is wrong."

### On leadership style
[一手] "A leader isn't good because they're right; they're good because they're willing to learn and to trust the people around them."

## 四、Virgin Blog (virgin.com — ongoing, 2000s–present)

**Source**: richard.virgin.com — Branson writes multiple posts per month. Available free. Voice is warm, first-person, conversational.

### On community (2024 post)
[一手] "Community means everything. More than 50 years later, I'm reminded every day of how networking and community remains vital for entrepreneurs."

### Decision filter
[一手] "My decision to create anything new is based on this instinct: 'If I create this, can it be better than what everybody else is doing? Can it make a real difference in the world? Can it be something that we can all be very proud of?'"

### On people management
[一手] "I have always believed that the way you treat your employees is the way they will treat your customers, and that people flourish when they are praised."

## 五、Fortune Magazine Interview (June 2023)

**Source**: fortune.com/2023/06/01/richard-branson-top-tips-advice-entrepreneurs-founders-startup-virgin-billionaire/

### On risk and failure
[一手] "Entrepreneurship is often a 'no risk, no reward' arena, so you've got to take calculated risks and realize it's okay if it doesn't always work out."
[一手] "The brave may not live forever, but the cautious do not live at all."

### On dyslexia and lateral thinking
[一手] "Lateral thinking and using creativity to solve problems drives innovation. Give people the permission to dream big, bring radical ideas to the table."

### On Virgin Orbit bankruptcy
[二手 paraphrase] Branson acknowledged the failure directly, maintained optimism, and used it to illustrate his "calculated risk" philosophy — sometimes the downside happens, and you absorb it.

## 六、"Screw It, Let's Do It" (2006)

**Source**: Virgin Books. Short book capturing his decision-making doctrine.

### Core doctrine
[一手] "You don't learn to walk by following rules. You learn by doing and by falling over."
[一手] "Screw it, let's do it" — his phrase for momentum over analysis. Not recklessness: he always checks the downside first. If he can survive the worst case, he acts.

## 七、Recurring Written Themes (cross-source pattern)

### 1. People before profit ⭐⭐⭐
Appears in every book, every major blog post. Not positioning — it is his actual management framework.
[一手] "Clients do not come first. Employees come first. If you take care of your employees, they will take care of the clients."

### 2. Every business exists to solve a problem ⭐⭐⭐
[一手] "Every business should be launched to solve a problem. If you can genuinely improve something that already exists, build it."

### 3. Fun is the signal ⭐⭐
[推断] If Branson is bored or something isn't fun, he exits. Virgin Cola failed partly because cola wasn't personally interesting beyond the stunt. Virgin Atlantic succeeded because he genuinely cared about the experience.

### 4. Failure is expected and public ⭐⭐
[一手] "We've had our share of failures." — said routinely, proactively, without being asked. Celebrating failures openly is central to his brand of authenticity.
