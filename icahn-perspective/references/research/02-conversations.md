## 一、Bloomberg TV 採訪（2022年2月16日，86歲生日）

**主持人**：Eric（主要）、Taylor Riggs、Romain Bostic
**背景**：Bloomberg TV現場，Icahn生日當天，主動接受採訪，狀態積極

### 論通脹與央行

[一手] "It doesn't end happily. And I've seen God knows how many bear markets and it is similar in one way. It's a sad thing. And a lot of people lose a great deal of capital."

[一手] "You can't keep printing up money and printing it up and printing it up because you get what you have now that you can't control."

[一手] "You know bring interest rates up to 18 percent. I remember those days. It was unbelievable."

[一手] "I'm not a macroeconomic guru. And I just look at simple facts. That's what it's all I do. Simple facts."

[一手] "You can't just keep giving away money and giving away money giving away money because that money will definitely lose its worth like anything else. If you make too many widgets and the widgets lose their work."

[推斷] Icahn用"widgets"（小零件）比喻貨幣，刻意降低學術感。他的宏觀觀點都用市井語言表達——這是刻意的。

### 論投資哲學與激進主義

[一手] "You have to buy things where the rest of the world is looking at you and thinking you're a little bit crazy. You're going against the trend."

[一手] "A lot of times, events are overblown. Overblown on the good side, overblown on the bad side."

[一手] "The paradigm that I find the best for investing...is activism and going into companies where the stock is selling cheaply for a number of reasons. And if we can get in there and we can do something we can help that company."

[一手] "A lot of companies in this country are terribly run just terribly run."

[一手] "We try to clean it up and just change a few simple things...We ended up since even since 2000...if you put money into IEP...you made 1900 percent. And I think the S&P is only 600 percent."

[一手] "The proof of the pudding is the eating."

[推斷] Icahn在訪談中反複用IEP作為論據（"proof of the pudding"）——績效記錄是他最強的防禦。

### 論尋找被低估公司

[一手] "When you say the stock is cheap it's cheap for us when we think the hidden value in the company."

[一手] "We just find companies that we think are valuable if you do certain things where you have poor managements and you can just uncover the hidden jewel for us."

[一手] "I try to stay away as much as I can...Stay away from predicting the near term movements of the market. I read all the guys and they all have great ideas. But I tell you I think there's just too many variables."

### 論CMBX / 商業地產空頭

[一手] "The CBX...it's sort of a mall short...it's analogous to...buying insurance."

[一手] "We have that insurance. We made a fair amount of money by buying that insurance on the malls."

[一手] "I believe that market is manipulated quite a bit...maybe to some extent it's legal maybe not."

[一手] "Mrs. Jones out in Iowa somewhere I think she's buying treasuries. Because what they do is tell people you know this is similar to treasuries."

[一手] "It's analogous to having insurance on your health...And you get sick. How would you like it if the insurance company basically...pay off the doctor or threaten the doctors to say the guy's not sick."

[推斷] 複雜的衍生品用醫療保險比喻——Icahn在解釋複雜金融工具時，總是找到最日常的類比。

### 論McDonald's豬隻待遇

[一手] "A pig is a nice you know pig has a good brain and it's a feeling animal."

[一手] "It almost sadistic. You put them in this crate for its whole life. A crate with a pig can't move."

[一手] "I called the CEO back. I said they're going to call me. And they called me up. And then I discussed what we said. I thought we agree with you Carl. And we agree."

[一手] "They just never delivered. They did a little something but never delivered."

[一手] "I'm not trying to be holier than thou or sanctimonious. But I said hey wait a minute. That's terrible what goes on."

[推斷] 動物權益是Icahn少數幾個非財務議題之一，且是出於私人情感（女兒的影響），不是計算出來的公關策略。語言比談商業時更鬆弛、更個人。

### 論Trump與政治

[一手] "I would have to think about that quite a bit. I think Trump did a lot of good things and I disagree with him on a number of things."

[一手] "I would support any Republican that's nominated for the presidency. The way this plays out today."

[一手] "I'm not sure it matters a hell of a lot who I support anyway."

[推斷] 政治話題是Icahn明顯不舒服的區域。他用"I don't want to get into politics"作防護，但立場明確——習慣性共和黨派，但不是盲目的。

## 二、CNBC Squawk Box系列（2012-2020）

[二手，via Verified Investing] Icahn最具代表性的電視表現：不被採訪者問題牽制，主動設置議題；在Apple/Dell/Netflix等campaign期間使用媒體作槓桿。

[推斷] CNBC訪談的特徵：聲音提高、不怕dead air、不接受對方的框架。他把每次採訪都變成"我的演講"。

**標題**：Icahn: The Restless Billionaire
**導演**：Bruce David Klein

[推斷] 紀錄片中的關鍵時刻：Icahn描述晚上打電話給目標公司CEO的習慣，這是他讓對方感受壓力的戰術之一。

## 四、訪談中的核心對話模式

### 模式一：把任何問題redirect回管理層問題
無論問通脹、政治還是個人生活，Icahn最終都會回到：「這個國家最大的問題是管理層不負責任。」

### 模式二：用IEP績效封殺質疑
[一手] "If you bought our stock IEP...you made 1900 percent."
這是他的"trump card"——不喜歡問題就拿出記錄。

### 模式三：日常類比解構複雜金融
Widgets、health insurance、Mrs. Jones in Iowa——所有複雜金融工具都有相對應的街頭比喻。

### 模式四：自我修正
[一手] "I'm not predicting a bear market...I'm only saying...over the next three four years..."
Icahn在公開場合刻意避免過度承諾，但結論仍是悲觀的。
