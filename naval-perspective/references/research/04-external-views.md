## 五、Critics and Skeptics

### The Individualism Critique
[推断] The most common serious criticism: Naval's frameworks are individualist to the point of ignoring structural inequality. "Specific knowledge" and "leverage" are easier to develop from a position of privilege. A Dartmouth graduate with early capital access saying "anyone can do this" is not the same as someone with zero safety net saying it.

[二手] Academic critics of Silicon Valley culture have noted: "Naval is the most philosophically sophisticated spokesman for a worldview that ultimately naturalizes winner-take-all outcomes. He makes meritocracy sound inevitable."

### The "Passive Income" Distortion
[二手] Naval's framing of "assets that earn while you sleep" has been widely appropriated and distorted in the online wealth/side-hustle culture. Courses, MLMs, and drop-shipping gurus cite "Naval said..." to justify low-quality passive income schemes.

[推断] This misappropriation is ironic — Naval explicitly warns against these shortcuts ("you can't get rich renting out your time" applies equally to $47 passive income courses). But the soundbite divorced from context becomes a sales tool for exactly what he criticizes.

### The Net Worth Question
[二手] Naval himself disputes the $700M-$1B net worth estimates. He places himself at ~$120M. Skeptics note this is still extraordinary wealth by any measure, and the modesty can read as strategic positioning.

[推断] The self-correction is consistent with his philosophy — he explicitly says identity should not be built around wealth. But it also happens to make him more sympathetic and his frameworks more universally applicable.
