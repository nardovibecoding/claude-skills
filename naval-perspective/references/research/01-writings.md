## 一、"How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)" — Core Tweetstorm
**Source**: nav.al/rich | Original Twitter thread: twitter.com/naval/status/1002103360646823936 | Posted May 31, 2018

### On Wealth vs. Money vs. Status
[一手] "Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep. Wealth is the factory with robots that's cranking out things. Wealth is the computer program that's running at night that's serving other customers. Wealth is even money in the bank that is being reinvested into other assets and into other businesses."

[一手] "But really the reason you want wealth is because it buys your freedom. So you don't have to wear a tie like a collar around your neck. So you don't have to wake up at 7 a.m. and rush to work and sit in commute traffic... The purpose of wealth is freedom. It's nothing more than that. It's not to buy fur coats or drive Ferraris or sail yachts. That stuff gets really boring and really stupid really fast. It's really just so that you are your own sovereign individual."

[一手] "Money is how we transfer wealth. Money is social credits. It is the ability to have credits and debits on other people's time."

[一手] "Wealth is not a zero sum game. Everybody in the world can have a house. Because you have a house doesn't take away from my ability to have a house. If anything the more houses that are built the easier it becomes to build houses... Status on the other hand is a zero sum game. It's a very old game. We've been playing it since monkey tribes."

[一手] "You should avoid status games in your life because they make you into an angry combative person. You're always fighting to put other people down."

### On Getting Rich — The Core Thesis
[一手] "You're not going to get rich renting out your time. You must own equity, a piece of a business to gain your financial freedom."

[一手] "Society will pay you for creating things that it wants. But society doesn't yet know how to create those things."

[一手] "In a thousand parallel universes, you want to be wealthy in 999 of them. You don't want to be wealthy in the 50 of them where you got lucky."

[一手] "I like to think that if I lost all my money and if you drop me on a random street in any English-speaking country, within 5 to 10 years I'd be wealthy again because it's just a skill set that I've developed and I think anyone can develop."

### On Specific Knowledge
[一手] "Specific knowledge is found much more by pursuing your innate talents, your genuine curiosity, and your passion. It's not by going to school for whatever is the hottest job."

[一手] "The first thing to notice about specific knowledge is that you can't be trained for it. If you can be trained for it, if you can go to a class and learn specific knowledge, then somebody else can be trained for it too. And then we can mass produce and mass train people."

[一手] "All of this stuff feels like play to me, but it looks like work to others."

[一手] "The first person to actually point out my real specific knowledge was my mother. She said it when I was like 15 or 16 years old. I was telling a friend that I wanted to be an astrophysicist. And she said, no, you're going to go into business... She had already observed that every time we walked down the street, I would critique the local pizza parlor on why they were selling their slices a certain way."

### On Leverage
[一手] "Arm yourself with specific knowledge, accountability and leverage."

[一手] "Tools and leverage are what create this disconnection between inputs and outputs."

[一手] "If you want to make money, you have to get paid at scale. And why you, that's accountability. At scale, that's leverage. And just you getting paid as opposed to somebody else getting paid, that's specific knowledge."

### On Long-Term Games
[一手] "All the benefits in life come from compound interests. Whether it's in relationships, or making money, or in learning."

[一手] "In a long-term game, it's positive sum. We're all baking the pie together. We're trying to make it as big as possible. In a short-term game, we're cutting up the pie."

[一手] "Play iterated games. All returns in life, whether in wealth, relationships or knowledge come from compound interest."

[一手] "Every time you reset, every time you wander out of where you built your network, you're going to be starting from scratch."

### On Building vs. Selling
[一手] "Learn to sell, learn to build. If you can do both, you will be unstoppable."

[一手] "There's a builder and there's a seller. There's a CEO and CTO combo. And venture and technology investors are almost trained to look for this combo whenever possible. It's the magic combination."

### On Capitalism and Wealth Creation
[一手] "I don't think capitalism is evil. Capitalism is actually good. It's just that it gets hijacked."

[一手] "Capitalism is not something we invented. Capitalism is not even something we discovered. It is innate to us in every exchange that we have."

[一手] "Today, I would rather be a poor person in a first world country than be a rich person in Louis XIV's France."

[一手] "The history of the world, in some sense, is this predator-prey relationship between makers and takers."

[一手] "If you get too many takers and not enough makers, society falls apart."

## 二、The Almanack of Naval Ravikant — Key Passages
**Source**: Eric Jorgenson (2020), compiled from tweets/interviews; navalmanack.com (free)

### On Wealth
[一手] "Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep. Money is how we transfer time and wealth. Status is your place in the social hierarchy."

[一手] "You're not going to get rich renting out your time. You must own equity — a piece of a business — to gain your financial freedom."

[一手] "Give society what it wants, but doesn't yet know how to get — at scale."

### On Happiness
[一手] "Happiness is a skill you can develop." [推断: framing happiness as learnable, not given]

[一手] "The most important trick to being happy is to realize happiness is a skill and a choice."

[一手] "A happy person isn't someone who's happy all the time. It's someone who effortlessly interprets events in such a way that nothing has the power to crush their inner happiness."

### On Reading
[一手] "The foundation of learning is reading. I don't know a smart person who doesn't read and read all the time."

[一手] "Read what you love until you love to read. It's that simple."

[一手] "Read the original books in a given field that are very scientific in their nature. Instead of reading a business book, pick up Adam Smith's The Wealth of Nations."

## 四、On Luck (Four Types Framework)
**Source**: "How to Get Rich" podcast/transcript

[一手] "There's really four kinds of luck. The first kind is blind luck, where I just got lucky because something completely out of my control happened... Then there's luck that comes through persistence, hard work, hustle, motion... A third way is that you just become very good at spotting luck... And then the last kind of luck is the weirdest, hardest kind — where you build a unique character, a unique brand, a unique mindset where then luck finds you."

[一手] "We make our fortunes and we call them fate." [二手: Benjamin Disraeli, quoted approvingly]

[一手] "Extreme people get extreme results." [二手: Sam Altman, quoted approvingly]

[一手] "You can't be normal and expect abnormal returns." [二手: Jeffrey Pfeffer, quoted approvingly]
