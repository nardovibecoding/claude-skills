## 一、Early Life (1974–1995)

**1974**
- [二手] Born in New Delhi, India. Exact birth date not publicly documented.
- [推断] Family circumstances described as modest; father's role not publicly discussed. Mother is the dominant parental figure in his account.

**~1983 (age ~9)**
- [二手] Immigrated to New York City with his mother. No extended family network.
- [推断] The New York Public Library becomes the primary educational and social refuge. Naval has cited this repeatedly as formative — books as the great equalizer, accessible regardless of financial situation.
- [一手] "I started as a poor kid in India."

**1983–1991 (New York)**
- [二手] Grew up in New York. The immigrant experience — navigating American culture, class systems, and meritocratic pathways — shapes his entire worldview.
- [推断] Early exposure to the gap between status games (school social hierarchies) and wealth creation (observing small businesses, critiquing them from age ~15).

**1991**
- [二手] Admitted to Stuyvesant High School — New York's elite public exam school, entry by competitive exam only. Selective, meritocratic.
- [推断] Stuyvesant attendance confirms Naval's early intellectual ability and willingness to compete on merit. The school produces a disproportionate number of tech founders.

**1991–1995**
- [二手] Dartmouth College; double major in Economics and Computer Science.
- [一手] "I wanted to be a scientist. My moral hierarchy was scientists at the top, then artists, then everyone else." [自述: wanted to be an astrophysicist]
- [一手] Mother told him at ~15: "No, you're going to go into business." She had observed him critiquing local businesses.
- [推断] The tension between scientific aspiration and business reality is the defining intellectual theme of Naval's 20s. He ultimately synthesizes both through technology investment.

## 二、First Career Phase (1995–2007)

**1995**
- [二手] Graduated Dartmouth. Enters tech during the pre-dot-com boom era.

**~1996–1998**
- [二手] Various early tech roles, details sparse. Begins angel investing informally.

**1999**
- [二手] Co-founded Epinions — consumer reviews platform.
- [推断] Epinions was genuinely innovative (predating Yelp by years) but failed to achieve dominance.
- [一手] "The second little fortune that I made, or I should have made, I got cheated by my business partners."
- [推断] The Epinions partner betrayal is the central formative failure. It didn't destroy him financially, but it permanently shaped his view on integrity as the non-negotiable filter for all relationships.

**~2000 (approximate)**
- [一手] "First little fortune that I made, I instantly lost in the stock market."
- [推断] Dot-com crash era. Many early stock gains evaporated. Naval lost his first significant capital in the crash.

**~2000–2006**
- [二手] Continued angel investing and startup work. Details of this period sparse.
- [推断] The recovery period after two early failures (stock loss + Epinions betrayal). Naval is building the framework from defeat.

**2007**
- [二手] Founded The Hit Forge — $20M angel fund. First systematic investment vehicle.
- [二手] Made early investments in Twitter, Uber, Stack Overflow during this period.
- [推断] Twitter investment (2007-2008 pre-product-market-fit) demonstrates early pattern recognition: communication infrastructure + network effects + asymmetric upside.

## 三、AngelList Era (2010–2018)

**2010**
- [二手] Co-founded AngelList with Babak Nivi.
- [一手] "AngelList was built because the system was fundamentally unfair — access to early-stage startups was gated by who you knew, not what you could contribute."
- [推断] AngelList is the positive-sum platform play that NavalList's philosophy demands: democratize access, create infrastructure, benefit from network effects without zero-sum competition.

**2010–2013**
- [二手] AngelList grows from startup database to active fundraising platform.
- [二手] Syndicates feature (2013) allows individual angels to lead deals with follower capital — directly empowers non-institutional angels.
- [推断] The Syndicates launch is when AngelList's systemic importance becomes undeniable.

**2014**
- [二手] Co-founded MetaStable Capital — one of the first institutional crypto hedge funds, with Lucas Ryan and Tushar Jain.
- [推断] Early institutional crypto bet, pre-mainstream VC adoption. Consistent with first-principles framework: if code is the highest-leverage asset, crypto assets (programmable money) represent an extreme case.

**~2014–2017**
- [二手] Naval steps back from day-to-day CEO role at AngelList.
- [推断] This is the "retirement" decision enacted: optimize for thinking time, not operational control. AngelList continues growing without Naval at the helm.

**2018 (May 31)**
- [二手] Posts "How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)" tweetstorm — 40+ tweets.
- [一手] "Yeah, the how to get rich tweet storm definitely hit a nerve. A lot of people say it was helpful, reach across aisles, people outside of the tech industry, people in all walks of life."
- [推断] The tweetstorm goes viral because it offers a framework, not tactics. It's addressed to everyone, not just SV insiders. This is when Naval becomes a public intellectual rather than just an investor.

## 四、Public Intellectual Phase (2018–Present)

**2018–2019**
- [二手] Naval publishes the "How to Get Rich" podcast series (nav.al) — ~3.5 hours of expanded content from the tweetstorm.
- [二手] Follows with "Naval" podcast on happiness — the second half of his framework.
- [推断] The two podcast series represent Naval's complete worldview: wealth creation (external) + happiness (internal). He treats them as equally important, sequenced deliberately.

**2018–2020**
- [二手] JRE #1309 (Joe Rogan, 2019) — most widely distributed interview. Reaches mainstream beyond tech.
- [二手] Farnam Street interview (Shane Parrish) — most philosophically rigorous.
- [推断] In this period, Naval transitions from "well-known in Silicon Valley" to "global public intellectual." The Almanack is the mechanism.

**2020**
- [二手] *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant* published by Eric Jorgenson — compiled from tweets, interviews, podcasts.
- [二手] Naval insisted it be freely downloadable (navalmanack.com). Available on Amazon, also free PDF.
- [二手] 1M+ copies distributed within first two years.
- [推断] The Almanack is Naval's legacy text. He didn't write it — Jorgenson compiled it — but Naval shaped its contents through what he approved and what he publicly said.

**2020–2026**
- [二手] AngelList continues scaling: $170B+ assets under management; 28% of all high-quality early-stage venture deals.
- [二手] Estimated net worth ~$120M (Naval disputes higher estimates).
- [推断] Naval in this phase: occasional tweet, rare interview, no new major platform. Consistent with his own stated philosophy: compound quietly, give away the framework, don't chase status.
