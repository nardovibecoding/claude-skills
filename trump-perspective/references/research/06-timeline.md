# Donald Trump — Chronological Timeline

> Research date: 2026-04-07
> Tags: [一手] = direct quotes | [二手] = reported/paraphrased | [推断] = inferred

---

## 一、Origins & Formation (1946–1973)

**1946** — Born June 14, Queens, New York City. Father: Fred Trump Sr. (outer-borough housing developer). Mother: Mary Anne MacLeod (Scottish immigrant).

**1950s–60s** — Raised in Jamaica Estates, Queens. Attended Kew-Forest School. Described as difficult, combative child. Sent to New York Military Academy at 13.

[二手] Fred Trump's instruction to young Donald: "Be a killer." The outer-borough Queens identity forms — wealthy enough to be educated, but excluded from Manhattan elite social circles.

**1964–1966** — Attends Fordham University for two years, then transfers to Wharton School of Finance, University of Pennsylvania.

**1968** — Graduates from Wharton. Receives medical deferment from Vietnam draft (bone spurs). Joins father's real estate company, Elizabeth Trump & Son.

**1971–1973** — Renames the company The Trump Organization. Begins shifting focus from outer-borough housing to Manhattan.

---

## 二、Rise (1973–1987)

**1973** — US Department of Justice sues Trump Organization for racial discrimination in housing rentals. Settles without admitting guilt in 1975.

**1977** — Marries Ivana Zelníčková (Czech model). They will have three children: Donald Jr. (1977), Ivanka (1981), Eric (1984).

**1978** — First major Manhattan project: renovation of the Commodore Hotel (renamed Grand Hyatt). First deal using government tax abatement — learned to negotiate with city government.

**1983** — Trump Tower opens on 5th Avenue at 57th Street. First building in gold letters. Penthouse as Trump's primary residence. The brand is born.

**1984–1985** — Expands to Atlantic City: Trump Plaza Hotel and Casino, Trump Castle.

**1987** — *The Art of the Deal* published. #1 NYT bestseller. Ghostwritten with Tony Schwartz. Creates Trump the business celebrity, not just Trump the developer.

[一手] "Deals are my art form. I like making deals, preferably big deals. That's how I get my kicks." (Art of the Deal, 1987)

---

## 三、Overextension & Collapse (1988–1995)

**1988** — Acquires the Plaza Hotel in New York for $407 million. Over-leveraged.

**1989** — Buys Eastern Air Shuttle, renamed Trump Shuttle. Sells at loss in 1992.

**1990** — Trump Taj Mahal opens in Atlantic City — largest casino in the world at the time. $1.3 billion construction cost, majority financed by junk bonds.

**1990–1991** — Multiple bankruptcies begin. Personal debt: $975 million personally guaranteed.

[一手] "I had owed billions and billions of dollars. $975 million or so was personally guaranteed. That's a pretty deep hole... it was hunker down time." (BBC HardTalk 1998)

**1991** — Divorces Ivana Trump after affair with Marla Maples. High-profile tabloid coverage.

**1993** — Marries Marla Maples. Daughter Tiffany born.

**1995** — "Survive till 95" philosophy pays off. Economy recovers. Trump's debts restructured by banks who can't afford to let him fail.

[一手] "I came up with a phrase: survive till 95. And it turned out to be right because the world changed." (BBC 1998)

---

## 四、Comeback & TV Era (1996–2015)

**1996** — Divorces Marla Maples.

**1998** — BBC HardTalk interview: Trump at 51, rebuilding. Claims his company is "much bigger now than it ever was." First glimpse of the "comeback" narrative that will define his political career.

[一手] "I really blame myself a little bit because I've always been able to pick markets. And I really wasn't focused toward the end of the 80s because I was having too good a time." (BBC 1998)

**2001** — Marries Melania Knauss (Slovenian model). Son Barron born 2006.

**2004** — *The Apprentice* premieres on NBC. Peak viewership 20–28 million. Trump plays decisive all-knowing boss. "You're fired" enters the culture.

[推断] The Apprentice is the single most important step in Trump's political career — it created his public image with the working-class audiences who would become his voters. The character was more powerful than the biography.

**2004** — Trump Hotels and Casinos Resorts files for Chapter 11 bankruptcy (fourth Trump bankruptcy filing).

**2005–2015** — The Trump brand expands: Trump University (sued for fraud, settles for $25 million 2016), Trump-branded hotels in multiple cities, Miss Universe pageant ownership, golf courses.

**2011** — Trump launches "birtherism" — publicly questioning Obama's birth certificate. First major political incursion. Gets massive media coverage.

[推断] Birtherism was Trump's test run: could a factually false, racially coded attack generate political energy? Answer: yes. It made him a Republican primary figure without running for anything.

## 五、Political Ascent (2015–2020)

**June 16, 2015** — Presidential announcement via escalator descent at Trump Tower. "Rapists and murderers" line about Mexican immigrants sets the rhetorical template.

**2015–2016** — Dominates Republican primary with a plurality of the vote. Defeats 16 opponents. Establishes attack nicknames: Lyin' Ted, Little Marco, Jeb!, Crooked Hillary.

[二手] Earns $2 billion in free media coverage vs. paid advertising. Never apologises for a single statement.

**July 2016** — Republican National Convention. "I alone can fix it" — the signature phrase of authoritarian self-sufficiency.

[一手] "I alone can fix it." — RNC acceptance speech, July 21, 2016.

**November 8, 2016** — Defeats Hillary Clinton. Electoral College: 306–232. Popular vote: loses by 2.9 million. Defies every major pollster and forecaster.

[推断] The 2016 win validated every instinct Trump had about politics: grievance is powerful, identity beats policy, the establishment is weaker than it looks, and the media's hostility helps rather than hurts.

**2017** — Tax Cuts and Jobs Act signed. Corporate rate drops from 35% to 21%. Largest tax reform in 30 years.

**2017** — Withdraws from Paris Climate Agreement and Trans-Pacific Partnership.

**2018–2019** — China trade war begins. Tariffs on $360 billion in Chinese goods.

**September 2019** — First impeachment (Ukraine/Zelensky call). Senate acquits.

**2020** — Abraham Accords: Israel-UAE, Israel-Bahrain normalisation. Most significant Middle East diplomatic outcome in a generation.

**2020** — COVID-19 pandemic. Trump's handling is widely criticised. Economy collapses. Approves Operation Warp Speed — vaccine developed in record time.

**November 3, 2020** — Loses to Joe Biden. Popular vote: loses by 7 million. Electoral College: 232–306.

## 六、Resistance, Indictment & Comeback (2021–2024)

**November–December 2020** — Refuses to concede. Launches "Stop the Steal" campaign. Files 60+ lawsuits challenging results — all fail.

**January 6, 2021** — Capitol riot. Trump watches on television for hours as supporters breach the Capitol. Impeached for "incitement of insurrection" — acquitted by Senate 57-43 (majority, not two-thirds required).

[推断] The conventional verdict: January 6 should have ended Trump. The actual result: he remained dominant in the Republican Party. The refusal to concede became his defining political act — validating his base's belief that the system is rigged against them.

**August 2022** — FBI raid on Mar-a-Lago, searching for classified documents.

**August 2023** — First criminal indictment: 37 counts federal, classified documents.

**2023** — Three more indictments: New York hush money payments, Georgia election interference, federal January 6 charges. Total: 91 felony counts.

[推断] Every indictment produced a polling bounce for Trump and a fundraising surge. He converted legal vulnerability into political proof of victimhood.

**January 2024** — Wins Iowa caucus with 51% — record margin.

**July 13, 2024** — Assassination attempt at Butler, Pennsylvania rally. Bullet grazes his ear. Raises fist from the ground — "Fight! Fight! Fight!" — the image defines the final phase of the campaign.

**November 5, 2024** — Defeats Kamala Harris. Electoral College: 312–226. Wins popular vote for first time — first Republican to do so since 2004.

[一手] "We overcame obstacles that nobody thought possible and it is now clear that we've achieved the most incredible political thing." (victory speech, Nov 2024)

## 七、Second Term (2025–2026)

**January 20, 2025** — Inaugurated as 47th President.

**Early 2025** — Mass deportation operations. Government shutdown with Democrats. Tariff escalation with China.

**2025** — China trade war de-escalation deal. Iran nuclear facilities struck by US B-2 bombers. Trump claims "Middle East peace for 3,000 years."

**2025** — Claims to have resolved 8 active conflicts globally (Cambodia/Thailand, Kosovo/Serbia, Congo/Rwanda, Pakistan/India, Israel/Iran, Egypt/Ethiopia, Armenia/Azerbaijan, Israel/Hamas).

[一手] "I solved eight of them... You know how I solved them? I said, if you don't stop fighting, I'm putting tariffs on both of your countries." (60 Minutes 2025)

**2025–2026** — Russia/Ukraine war remains unresolved. Trump characterises it as "Biden's war."

[一手] "That was a war that would have never happened if I was president... That's Joe Biden's war, not my war. I inherited that stupid war." (60 Minutes 2025)

## 八、Timeline Summary: The Three Trumps

| Era | Period | Identity | Core Achievement |
|-----|--------|----------|-----------------|
| The Developer | 1973–1987 | Builder, brand-creator | Trump Tower, Art of the Deal |
| The Survivor | 1988–2003 | Comeback story | Bankruptcy → The Apprentice |
| The Politician | 2015–present | Populist nationalist | 45th & 47th President, political movement |

[推断] The through-line across all three eras: **never concede**. Trump lost $975 million personally and came back. He lost the presidency in 2020 and came back. The comeback narrative is the deepest truth of his career — more powerful than any individual policy or deal.
