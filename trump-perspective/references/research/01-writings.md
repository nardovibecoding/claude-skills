> **Research note**: Trump's primary written output is The Art of the Deal (1987, ghostwritten with Tony Schwartz), supplemented by social media posts, executive orders, and rally transcripts. Unlike most politicians, his books read like extended sales pitches — first-person, boastful, transactional.

## 一、The Art of the Deal (1987) — Core Framework

**Background**: #1 NYT bestseller. Ghostwritten with Tony Schwartz, who has since repudiated the book and called Trump "a sociopath." Trump considers it his defining text — has called it "the best business book ever written, probably." Published when Trump was 41, peak of his 1980s brand ascent.

### Deal Philosophy
[一手] "Deals are my art form. I like making deals, preferably big deals. That's how I get my kicks."

[一手] "I aim very high, and then I just keep pushing and pushing and pushing to get what I'm after."

[一手] "My style of deal-making is quite simple and straightforward. I aim very high."

### The Trump Cards (Chapter 2 — Deal Principles)
[一手] **Think Big**: "Most people think small because they're afraid of success, afraid of making decisions, afraid of winning."

[一手] **Protect the Downside**: "Protect the downside and the upside will take care of itself." — Plan for worst case; upside handles itself.

[一手] **Maximize Options**: Never lock yourself into one path. Keep multiple irons in the fire.

[一手] **Use Your Leverage**: "The worst thing you can possibly do in a deal is seem desperate to make it. That makes the other guy smell blood, and then you're dead."

[一手] **Get the Word Out**: Publicity is everything. "Good publicity is preferable to bad, but from a bottom-line perspective, bad publicity is sometimes better than no publicity. Controversy, in short, sells."

[一手] **Fight Back**: "I believe in an eye for an eye. If somebody has hurt you, if somebody's gone out of their way to hurt you, I think if you have the opportunity, you should certainly go out of your way to do a number on them." (also confirmed in BBC HardTalk 1998)

[一手] **Deliver the Goods**: "If you can't deliver, sooner or later the game is up."

[推断] The book is Trump's operating manual. It functions less as autobiography and more as manifesto — every chapter teaches a version of the same lesson: dominate, leverage, never show vulnerability.

## 二、Victory Speech — November 2024

**Background**: Trump's second victory speech, delivered after winning the popular vote in addition to the Electoral College — the first Republican to do so in 20 years.

[一手] "We overcame obstacles that nobody thought possible and it is now clear that we've achieved the most incredible political thing."

[一手] "We have a country that needs help, and it needs help very badly. We're going to fix our borders, we're going to fix everything about our country."

[一手] "I will be fighting for you with every breath in my body."

[一手] "The golden age of America." (his framing for the second term vision)

[一手] Description of coalition as: "Young and old, men and women, rural and urban... Union, nonunion, African American, Hispanic American, Asian American, Arab American, Muslim American, we had everybody."

[推断] The victory speech establishes his governing frame: "historic realignment," "common sense," and the promise of restoration. Note the absence of specific policy details — the speech is entirely emotional and identity-based.

## 三、Social Media / Public Statements Patterns

**Note**: Trump's Twitter/Truth Social output is the largest corpus of his direct voice outside rally transcripts. Key characteristics:

[一手, pattern] Nuclear testing post, hours before meeting Xi Jinping (2025): "instructed the Department of War to start testing our nuclear weapons immediately" — used as negotiating pressure signal.

[推断] Trump treats social media as a deal tool — public statements are negotiating moves, not policies. The nuclear post before the Xi meeting is a textbook example: create public pressure/uncertainty, then walk into negotiation with leverage.

### Recurring Written Themes
[推断] Across all public statements and writings:
1. Historical superlatives — "the most incredible," "like never before," "nobody's ever done it"
2. Attribution of past failures to predecessors (Obama, Biden)
3. Self-referential proof — "I did it," "everyone said it couldn't be done"
4. Numbers deployed for credibility: "$17 trillion," "11,888 murderers," "55-year low"

## 四、Rally Rhetoric — Written Patterns (Erie PA, Goodyear AZ, Waco TX)

**Structure of a Trump rally speech**:
[二手, via Rev.com transcripts] Average 9,000–15,000 words when transcribed. No prepared structure. Stream-of-consciousness from boast to attack to anecdote to grievance.

[一手, Erie PA] On economic record: "In my first three years, we increased family income over $6,000" — then pivot: "Why the hell didn't he do it? Why didn't he do it?" (incredulous rhetorical question targeting opponent)

[一手, Erie PA] Cascade attack: "Joe Biden is a corrupt politician" → "smoking gun emails" → "the Biden family sold the Vice Presidency" → "The media, the big tech giants, the Washington swamp and the Democrat party is a part of all of them, every single one of them."

[推断] The rally script is: boast → incredulous question → institutional attack → crowd confirmation. It repeats with variation for 60–90 minutes. The crowd response ("Lock her up," "Say it!") is built in as punctuation.
