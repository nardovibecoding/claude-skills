# Warren Buffett — Primary Source Writings & Annual Letters

> Agent research output | Research date: 2026-04-07

>
> **Research note**: Buffett's annual letters to Berkshire Hathaway shareholders (1965–present) are his definitive written voice. Freely available at berkshirehathaway.com. Unlike most CEOs, he writes them himself — no PR team, no speechwriter. The letters are addressed to his "partners" (originally) and "shareholders" (later), framed as if explaining to a smart sibling. Carol Loomis of Fortune has edited nearly every letter since 1977.

## 一、年度股东信核心语录

### 论价格与价值
[一手] "Price is what you pay. Value is what you get." — 2008 letter; most cited Buffett quote

[一手] "It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price." — recurring theme; See's Candies was the turning point

[一手] "Whether we're talking about socks or stocks, I like buying quality merchandise when it is marked down." — classic Buffett everyman framing

### 论恐惧与贪婪
[一手] "Be fearful when others are greedy and greedy only when others are fearful." — 2004 letter; most repeated aphorism

[一手] "The stock market is a device for transferring money from the impatient to the patient." — attribution via multiple letters/interviews

### 论市场先生 (Mr. Market)
[一手] "The market is there to serve you and not to instruct you." — paraphrasing Ben Graham's Chapter 8, Intelligent Investor; repeated at Berkshire AGM

[一手] "Think of market fluctuations as your friend rather than your enemy; profit from folly rather than participate in it."

### 论耐心与等待
[一手] "If they are in the strike zone at all, the business 'pitches' we now see are just catching the lower outside corner. If we swing, we will be locked into low returns. But if we let all of today's balls go by, there can be no assurance that the next ones we see will be more to our liking." — 1997 letter; Ted Williams strike zone metaphor

[一手] "The trick is, when there is nothing to do, do nothing." — attributed multiple sources

### 论汉堡与股价逻辑 (The Hamburger Logic)
[一手] "If you plan to eat hamburgers throughout your life and are not a cattle producer, should you wish for higher or lower prices for beef? Likewise, if you are going to buy a car from time to time but are not an auto manufacturer, should you prefer higher or lower car prices?" — 1997 letter; long-term savers should welcome market declines

### 论鸭子与牛市 (The Preening Duck)
[一手] "Any investor can chalk up large returns when stocks soar, as they did in 1997. In a bull market, one must avoid the error of the preening duck that quacks boastfully after a torrential rainstorm, thinking that its paddling skills have caused it to rise in the world." — 1997 letter; self-deprecating wit about a "merely good" year

His final word on that year's performance: simply "Quack."

### 论管理质量
[一手] "I try to buy stock in businesses that are so wonderful that an idiot can run them. Because sooner or later, one will." — widely quoted; multiple letters

[一手] "Never deal with a rascal under the expectation that you can prevent him from cheating you." — 2023 letter; on character in business partners

### 论错误承认
[一手] "The cardinal sin is delaying the correction of mistakes. Problems cannot be wished away."

[一手] "I've made plenty of mistakes. The good news is they're mostly mistakes of omission rather than commission." — variations across multiple letters

### 论 Berkshire 架构与 Munger
[一手] "Charlie is the 'architect' of the present Berkshire, and I have been the 'general contractor.'" — 2023 letter; on Munger's philosophical role

Munger's instruction to Buffett: "Forget about ever buying another company like Berkshire. Instead, add to it wonderful companies purchased at fair prices." This reorientation — from Graham's pure asset-value approach to franchise/brand value — transformed Berkshire.

### 论会计与真实数字
[一手] "The primary difference between the mandated figures and the ones Berkshire prefers is that we exclude unrealized capital gains or losses that at times can exceed $5 billion a day." — 2023 letter; on GAAP vs. operating earnings

[一手] "There remain only a handful of companies in this country capable of truly moving the needle at Berkshire, and they have been endlessly picked over by us and by others." — 2023 letter; on the constraint of scale

### 论声誉
[一手] "It takes 20 years to build a reputation and 5 minutes to ruin it. If you think about that, you'll do things differently."

## 二、写作风格特征

**写作格言** (from multiple style analyses):
- Tone: Folksy teacher explaining to a bright 12-year-old
- Opens each letter with performance recap — admitting bad years with the same candor as good ones
- No bullet points. Meaty discursive paragraphs. Typically 10–20 pages
- Addresses shareholders as "partners" in spirit
- Never names competitors disparagingly
- Admits mistakes openly, often in detail
- Recurring sign-off warmth: "Charlie and I..."

[二手] "Buffett's annual letter is the most-read document in American investing. More people learn investing from his prose than from any textbook." — Globe and Mail analysis

[推断] The letters are not just investor communication — they are a sustained 60-year argument for a philosophy of life. Reading them chronologically reveals a man who got richer but didn't change his mind about what matters.

## 三、深度引述：从年度信中提炼的哲学宣言

### 论阅读与知识复利
[一手] "Read 500 pages every day. That's how knowledge works. It builds up like compound interest." — attributed via multiple interviews and letters

[推断] He reportedly reads 500–1,000 pages daily; 80% of his day is reading. The annual letters themselves are evidence of this — they are dense with historical reference, business case study, and original analogy. No ghostwriter could produce prose this specific.

### 论汉堡的经济学（完整逻辑链）
[一手] "If you plan to eat hamburgers throughout your life and are not a cattle producer, should you wish for higher or lower prices for beef? Likewise, if you are going to buy a car from time to time but are not an auto manufacturer, should you prefer higher or lower car prices? Now, if you expect to be a net saver during the next five years, should you hope for a higher or lower stock market during that period?" — 1997 letter

The implication: those who will buy stocks over decades should rejoice at falling prices. The letter makes explicit what feels counterintuitive to most investors.

### 论 See's Candies 的精神遗产
[推断] The See's Candies acquisition ($25 million, 1972) is the most philosophically important transaction in Buffett's history. It generated ~$2 billion in pre-tax earnings for Berkshire from 1972 to 2007, and it taught him that the right measure of a business is its earnings power, not its asset value. His letter discussion of See's established the "wonderful business at a fair price" framework that governs all subsequent Berkshire acquisitions.

### 论 Berkshire 的永久资本结构
[一手] "Berkshire shareholders need not worry about large losses from a few huge mistakes. We will always keep ample cash at Berkshire." — variation across multiple letters

[一手] "We are never going to risk what we have and need for what we don't have and don't need." — AGM; principle stated consistently in letters

### 论成功的真实衡量
[一手] "I measure success by how many people you want to love you actually do love you. Money is a by-product of doing what you love."

[推断] This is not a throwaway line — it recurs across decades of letters and interviews and represents a coherent alternative to the standard financial-industry definition of success. He lives by it: same house, same city, same friends, same McDonald's order.

## 四、反复出现的核心论点

### 1. 买企业，不买股票 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
[一手] "I never bought another stock. I bought businesses that happened to be publicly traded. But I became an owner of a business, and I did not care whether a stock went up or down the next day or the next week or the next month or the next year." — Charlie Rose interview

### 2. 能力圈原则 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
[一手] "Risk comes from not knowing what you're doing." — attributed via multiple sources

[一手] "The truth is, if we can't make a decision in five minutes, we can't make it in five months. We're not going to learn enough in the following five months to make up for the fact that we went in deficient in the first place." — AGM Q&A

### 3. 永不借钱冒险 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
