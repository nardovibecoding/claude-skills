## 一、早年：克羅地亞 (1962-1970)

**1962年9月14日** — 出生於Varaždin，南斯拉夫社會主義聯邦共和國（今克羅地亞）

**童年村莊生活**
[一手] "I grew up in a village back home and we had dirt floors, pigs running around, no phone or paved streets… but I didn't know we were poor." (Waterfront Magazine)

[一手] "I grew up on a small village with my grandma. Like the traditional vision of poverty with dirt floors. We didn't have a phone. There was one phone in the village. If you want to get a hold of my grandmother, you called the guy with the phone." (School of Greatness, 2025)

**父親被囚** (1960年代末)
[二手，via CNBC 2018] 父親因公開批評南斯拉夫政府而被投入監獄。出獄後，家庭決定離開，永遠不能回頭。

[一手] "My dad was in jail, could never go back." (School of Greatness, 2025)

## 二、移民加拿大 (1970年，8歲)

**抵達Halifax**
[一手] "It was my mom and dad escaping from a country, coming to Canada on a boat with one suitcase." (School of Greatness, 2025)

[二手] 家庭攜帶$20和一個行李箱抵達Halifax，在一個朋友的地下室住了18個月，然後遷往多倫多。

**第一天上學**
[一手] "I remember the first day of school and kids picking on me, making fun of me and telling me I'm a poor immigrant. First day of school. Because you don't speak English either at this point, right? And there was no English classes. So they just threw you in. And that must have been kind of terrifying."

**移民心態的形成 (約12歲)**
[一手] "I didn't realize that we were poor until we came to Canada."

[一手] "By 12, I'd gone to like three different high schools and my dad worked unbelievable amount of hours, like two shifts."

[一手] "At 12, I'd been living like that for four years. I was just like, I'm never going to get out of this cycle."

## 三、青年：尋找方向 (1970年代末-1980年代初)

**York University**
[二手，via research.md] 就讀York University，主修電影與英語文學。沒有商科背景。

[一手] "I have a degree in classic language literature." (School of Greatness, 2025)

**Harry Rosen男裝店工作 (大學期間)**
[一手] "I went there to buy a suit. It was like $1,200. And I can't afford it. The salesman said, if you work here, you get to buy one suit every six months at 50% off. So I got a part-time job there."

[一手] "Harry used to teach on Saturdays if you showed up an hour before the store opened. He would teach you everything you need to know about how to dress and how to sell to men. I show up. Nobody there. No one else shows up."

[一手] "He said, no one's paying me to show up. I always thought, I don't need to get paid to learn. I'll pay to learn. From the best."

[推斷] 這個Harry Rosen故事是他最重要的formative experience之一——它建立了"學習本身有價值"的心態，以及後來的"免費工作換入場"的策略邏輯。

**Avis Rent-A-Car工作**
[一手] "I worked for Warren Avis, who started Avis Rent-A-Car. I've just been really fortunate to have great role models who are good human beings." (School of Greatness, 2025)

## 四、科技業入場與BRAK Systems (1980年代末-2000年)

**免費工作換入場 (1980年代末)**
[二手] 沒有科技背景，說服一家科技公司讓他免費工作六個月。同時晚上在餐廳做服務員維持生計。

[推斷] 這建立了他的核心商業原則：如果你相信自己，就移除對方的風險。

**Tony Robbins研討會 (約26-27歲)**
[一手] "I went to a Tony Robbins seminar. I bought the super expensive ticket. Tony walks in. What I realized was all my energy was internal. I saw Tony and I realized I need to make other people better."

[一手] "I started changing our sales pitch to our customers away from product focus to a subconscious focus. I started thinking about why people buy and not what they're buying." (School of Greatness, 2025)

**因被解僱創立BRAK Systems (1990年代初)**
[一手] "I never wanted to be an entrepreneur. I just wanted to get a good job. I only became an entrepreneur because I got fired, and I needed to pay my mortgage." (Waterfront Magazine)

[二手，via Fortune 2016 & research.md] 創立BRAK Systems，一家加拿大互聯網安全軟件集成商。

**出售BRAK Systems給AT&T Canada，約$30.2M (約2000年，34-35歲)**
[一手] "I sold my first company to AT&T for $32 million. More money than I could have imagined. I was 34, 35."

[一手] "AT&T does the due diligence. They say the metrics aren't great. I said, did you ask those customers if they would buy from me again? 97% said yes. Two reasons: you made them happy, and they believed if they had an issue you would take care of them." (School of Greatness, 2025)

## 五、Herjavec Group的建立 (2003-今)

**創立Herjavec Group (2003年)**
[二手，via research.md] AT&T收購後數年，與同事以$20,000初始投資創立Herjavec Group。

**全押托管安全服務 (2007年)**
[推斷] 在大多數IT公司仍以產品銷售為主時，他把公司全面轉向托管安全服務訂閱模式——比競爭對手早了約五年。

**公司成長**
[二手，via CEO Magazine & research.md] Herjavec Group成長至$200M+年收入，成為加拿大最大的IT安全公司之一。在業界被視為genuine operator而非電視人物。

## 七、個人危機與重建 (2014年)

**2014年：抑鬱與自殺念頭**
[一手] "2014. I was going through a really, really difficult time. Like to the point I didn't think the sun would rise again. And like suicidal thinking. Contemplated suicide. Came close."

[一手] "I was going through a horrible divorce and just family stuff. I was a shell of what I was. I was void of me."

**Seattle流浪漢收容所 (2014年)**
[一手] "A good friend of mine, John, he's a priest. He said to me, I need you to go somewhere. I flew to Seattle on my super fancy plane and he took me to a homeless mission. I spent the next month or so there."

[一手] "Being in service for a month to the homeless in that city. Absolutely transformational."

[一手] "I went there broken and I left there like, whoo! Getting back to my faith, getting back to seeing value in me."

**信仰重建**
[一手] "Started out very religious, altar boy for many years. Became very cynical about it. And then went through a horrific time and found my faith again." (School of Greatness, 2025)
