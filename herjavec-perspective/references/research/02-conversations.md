## 一、School of Greatness Podcast — Lewis Howes (2025年2月)

**性質**：迄今最長、最坦誠的訪談。約90分鐘。覆蓋：移民心態、財富觀、2014年個人危機（含自殺念頭）、信仰重建。他在這裡說的話在任何其他公開平台都找不到。

### 論貧困是情境，不是身份
[一手] "Poverty and wealth are situational. So somebody listening to this could be making 50 grand a year, but they're a high power, high value individual."

[一手] "I think poverty is a learned mindset, and I think abundance is a learned mindset. I didn't learn a wealth mindset until later on."

[一手] "The beginning for me is a poverty mindset is when you believe you're poor. Or you believe that you are the cause of the situation you're in. Or you believe that external circumstances have made you what you are. I never believed I was poor. I just believed our current situation was poor."

### 論童年移民經歷
[一手] "It was my mom and dad escaping from a country, coming to Canada on a boat with one suitcase, and I'm the only child. For them, absolutely, horrifically hard. Didn't speak the language, had no money, my dad was in jail, could never go back. Now, I'm eight years old, first time on a boat. It's an adventure."

[一手] "I remember the first day of school and kids picking on me, making fun of me and telling me I'm a poor immigrant."

[一手] "I didn't realize that we were poor until we came to Canada."

### 論父親
[一手] "My dad worked in a factory his whole life, two shifts. Paid off his house in his lifetime. Super successful guy in his world. I will never work a day in my life harder than my dad worked. Ever. That man was the hardest working human being I'd ever come across."

### 論勤奮 vs. 聰明工作
[一手] "Just because you'll work hard though, doesn't mean you'll be rich. What it means is, you won't be poor. And there's a fundamental difference between the blue collar poverty mindset and the wealth abundance mindset."

[一手] "Markets and people will pay for value, not for time. People will pay you for your knowledge, not for how long you've been doing it."

### 論銷售的本質
[一手] "Good salespeople sell features. Great salespeople sell outcomes. And really great salespeople sell feelings."

[一手] "People never remember what you say. They remember how you make them feel."

[一手] "You can't force somebody to buy something they fundamentally don't need."

[一手] "I'm not wealthy because of my knowledge of a task. I'm wealthy because of my knowledge of sales."

### AT&T收購時的頓悟
[一手] "When I sold my first company to AT&T for $32 million... AT&T does the due diligence. They say, we measured your customer response and it's not great. I said, did you ask those customers if they would buy from me again? 97% of your customers said they would buy from you again, despite all of these things. Why? Two reasons. One, you made them happy. And two, they believed if they had an issue, you would take care of them."

### 論億萬富翁的三個特質
[一手] "Number one knee jerk is absolute obstinate obsession about their skill set. Not about the world in general, but about that one thing. General knowledge doesn't get rewarded. It's just a specific knowledge."

[一手] "The second one would be curiosity. It's hard to be abundant if you're not curious."

[一手] "The third one would be discipline. Discipline to me is fit body, fit mind, fit business."

### 論財富的定義演變
[一手] "When I sold my last company, in my mind I had a G4. And I used to think, oh, if I had a G6. Then I've made it. So I sold the business and within two weeks, I bought a G6."

[一手] "There is no car I want to buy, or no jet I need to buy, or no home I need to buy to fulfill me. What I need is I need to feel like I've drained every part of my potential. I want to feel like when I'm on my deathbed that I say I couldn't have done more."

[一手] "I think in terms of potential. So when I think I haven't made it, I don't think I've reached my potential."

### 論自我欺騙
[一手] "The biggest lie I've been telling myself lately is that I've made it. I got to a point where I felt like I've kind of made it. And then one day I'm like, hang on a sec. Age is a number. And I have more capital than I've ever had. So, yeah. I think I can still do it again."

[一手] "I've always been brutally honest with myself. I've never had an ego for success. So if somebody said something to me and there's even an ounce of truth to it, I would take it in. I would leave my ego at the door."

### 論2014年個人危機與信仰
[一手] "2014. I was going through a really, really difficult time. Like, I mean, like to the point I didn't think the sun would rise again. And like suicidal thinking. Yeah. Like contemplate suicide. Came close."

[一手] "I was going through a horrible divorce and just family stuff and like just horrible. And John, thank God, he's a priest. He said to me, I need you to go somewhere. And you have to have faith in me to trust me to go here. And I flew to Seattle on my super fancy plane and got off the plane. And he took me to a homeless mission."

[一手] "I spent the next month or so there. Being in service for a month to the homeless in that city. Absolutely transformational."

[一手] "I went there broken and I left there like, whoo!"

[一手] "I was void of me. Going there was a wake-up call and getting back to my faith, getting back to seeing value in me."

### 論信仰與目的
[一手] "People don't understand that faith doesn't mean that you give up control of your life to someone else. Like, I hate when people say that, oh, my life is in God's hands. And I'm like, yeah, but you got to do something."

[一手] "What do I ask God for today? Peace. I pray for peace. I pray for joy. I just want my family to feel fulfilled."

### 論軍人與企業家的差異
[一手] "People join the military because they have a need to do something greater than themselves. You're willing to lay your life down for our country. So then they leave the military. They get a job in sales. And their boss says, if you don't make your number, we're not going to make it for the quarter. And they struggle with that. They're void of purpose."

## 二、Grant Cardone Podcast (2022年10月)

**主題**：移民心態的局限，以及如何突破父母的謹慎哲學。

[一手] "As immigrants, you know what you're taught — don't tell the world about yourself. My parents were always like, if you're good at what you do, the world will beat a path to your door."

[推斷] 這是他最直接的"反移民謙遜"論述。他解釋了為什麼他必須主動顛覆父母的世界觀才能成功。
