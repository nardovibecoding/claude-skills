# cyber-streetwear — Off-White/Yeezy/Balenciaga digital

Cultural commitment: post-internet luxury. Monospace, redacted boxes, Helvetica capitals, neon-on-black.

## Allowed visual moves

- Helvetica or Akzidenz-Grotesk in CAPS for headers.
- Monospace metadata strips (timestamps, lot numbers, SKUs).
- Redacted black boxes over text (▮▮▮ aesthetic).
- Quotation marks around generic words ("PRODUCT", "SHOP", "INFO").
- Neon accent on near-black background — single highlight color.
- ALL CAPS lockups, stretched type, oversized letterspacing.
- Glitch frames, scan-line distortion (subtle).
- Catalog-page imagery — flat, unstyled, harsh-lit.

## Forbidden moves

- Soft warm palettes.
- Rounded corners.
- Hero photography with "lifestyle" feel.
- Customer-friendly onboarding.
- Help tooltips.
- Animations beyond glitch / scroll-jack.

## Example brands matching this register

- off-white, yeezy.com, balenciaga, ssense, hbx, end-clothing, virgil-abloh-archive, palace, supreme

## Sample copywriting voice

- "PRODUCT 014 / SS26 / LOT 0029"
- "FOR SHOPPING."
- "▮▮▮ NEW ARRIVALS ▮▮▮"
- "EU 42 — TWO PAIRS ONLY"

ALL CAPS norm. Quotation marks ironic. Inventory-numbers as flex. No marketing prose.

## Pairing

- Compatible directions: `brutalist` (best), `tech-utility` (dark variant).
- Compatible archetypes: `landing`, `hero`, `pricing` (lookbook variant).
- Avoid: `consumer-playful`, `civic-formal`, `craft-artisan`.

## Cultural anti-pattern

If the design feels like a startup with edgy fonts — drift. Cyber-streetwear is luxury catalog with hacker syntax, not "we're cool too" marketing.
