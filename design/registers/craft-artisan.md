# craft-artisan — Hermès/Aesop

Cultural commitment: hand-feel, restraint, fewer features-shown. Generous whitespace. Caslon, Söhne. Process-as-product.

## Allowed visual moves

- Generous whitespace (sections breathe at 120-160px vertical).
- Hand-feel typography: Caslon, Söhne, Domaine Display.
- Off-white backgrounds (#fafaf7 territory).
- Single muted accent — desaturated.
- Process photography (workshop, materials, tools — not product-on-white).
- Captions in italic with origin attribution.
- Single-product-per-page approach — no "more like this" upsells.
- Materials/origin lists ("100% combed Egyptian cotton, woven in Como").

## Forbidden moves

- Sales banners.
- "Limited edition" overuse (real scarcity, not fake).
- Too many products on one page.
- "Add to cart" prominent (subtle is correct).
- Animated transitions.
- Customer review carousels.
- AI-generated illustrations.

## Example brands matching this register

- hermes, aesop, le-labo, claude (anthropic), apple-environment-pages, dieline-craft-features, smythson, blackwell-publishers

## Sample copywriting voice

- "Made by Marie-Claire, in the Lyon atelier."
- "Cut, sewn, and finished entirely by hand. 14 hours per piece."
- "Ingredients: Tunisian neroli, Mediterranean lavender, sandalwood from Mysore."
- "Two new shades. Same recipe."

Specific. Place-name precise. Process-cited. No sales urgency.

## Pairing

- Compatible directions: `soft-warm` (best), `editorial-monocle`.
- Compatible archetypes: `landing`, `hero`, `footer`.
- Avoid: `dashboard`, `popup`, `chat`, `consumer-playful`.

## Cultural anti-pattern

If "shop now" appears above the fold — drift. Craft-artisan trusts the reader to discover commerce on their own pace.
