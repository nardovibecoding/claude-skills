# luxury-editorial — Kering/LVMH energy

Cultural commitment: heritage prestige, slow pacing, materials over features. Asymmetric grids. Warm whites. Serif display.

## Allowed visual moves

- Serif display at oversize (96-180px headlines).
- Asymmetric grid (golden ratio columns, hanging captions).
- Warm whites (#faf7f2 territory) — not pure #fff.
- Generous letter-spacing on small caps labels (0.1-0.2em).
- Full-bleed editorial photography. Composed, not stock.
- Italic for emphasis — never bold.
- Roman numerals for section markers.
- Black-on-cream OR cream-on-black. NO heavy color.

## Forbidden moves

- Sans-serif headlines.
- "Buy now" CTAs (use "Discover" / "Request" / "Explore").
- Discount banners. Ever.
- Animated gradients. Loops. Glow effects.
- Emoji.
- Tooltips. Toasts. Floating chat widgets.
- Stock product photography on white.

## Example brands matching this register

- hermes, kering, lvmh, aesop, blackwell, rimowa, bottega-veneta, dieline-style editorial, the-gentlewoman

## Sample copywriting voice

- "A study in restraint."
- "Cut from a single piece of leather, in the workshop in Florence."
- "Available by appointment."
- "Volume IV — Spring."

Long sentences allowed. Periods, not commas. Past tense narration. No exclamation marks ever.

## Pairing

- Compatible directions: `editorial-monocle` (best), `soft-warm`.
- Compatible archetypes: `landing`, `hero`, `footer`.
- Avoid: `tech-utility`, `dashboard`, `popup`.

## Cultural anti-pattern

If your hero says "Premium quality" or "Crafted with passion" — drift. Specificity (city, workshop name, year founded, material origin) replaces adjectives.
