# academic — JSTOR/MIT Press

Cultural commitment: scholarly authority. Footnote density. Computer Modern or IBM Plex Serif. No animation.

## Allowed visual moves

- Computer Modern, IBM Plex Serif, Charter, EB Garamond.
- Two-column print layout for long-form.
- Footnotes (numbered superscripts, hanging at page bottom or in margin).
- Chapter numerals (Roman or formatted §).
- Pull-quote margins for citations.
- Tables with thin rules, no row striping.
- Greyscale + 1 muted accent (deep navy, oxblood, forest green).

## Forbidden moves

- Sans-serif body.
- Animated transitions, hover lifts.
- Emoji.
- Gradient anything.
- Toast notifications, modals, popovers.
- Stock photos.
- Onboarding tooltips ("Welcome! Let's get started").

## Example brands matching this register

- jstor, mit-press, oxford-academic, cambridge-core, distill-pub, are-na (academic mode), notion-academic, papers (Readcube), quanta-magazine

## Sample copywriting voice

- "We argue that X."
- "See §3.2 for derivation."
- "The data, plotted in Figure 4, reveal..."
- "Submit revision by 15 March."

Passive voice tolerated. Citations inline (Smith, 2024). Hedge words OK ("may", "suggests", "appears to").

## Pairing

- Compatible directions: `editorial-monocle` (best), `modern-minimal` toned down.
- Compatible archetypes: `landing` (paper-style), `chart`, `table`, `footer`.
- Avoid: `glassmorphism`, `chat`, `popup`.

## Cultural anti-pattern

If you see "Get started in 60 seconds" or onboarding gamification — drift. Academic respects the reader's time enough to not patronize.
