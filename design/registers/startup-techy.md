# startup-techy — Vercel/Linear/Cursor energy

Cultural commitment: SF/SV product-launch energy. Dark default. Mono accents. Motion is OK. Tech-as-craft, not enterprise-as-bureaucracy.

## Allowed visual moves

- Geist Mono for code, badges, version pills.
- Dark default, light optional secondary.
- Subtle motion: 150-250ms ease-out on hover, fade-in on scroll.
- Hairline borders, single accent (electric blue / electric green / muted purple).
- Inline code blocks with syntax highlighting baked into hero copy.
- Small "v0.1.4" version stamps in nav.
- Keyboard-shortcut hints in UI (`⌘K`, `⌘/`).
- Animated gradients in hero — IF subtle. Mesh > linear.

## Forbidden moves

- Stock photos of "diverse team smiling".
- "Get Started" overuse — prefer "Try it" / "Open" / "Run".
- Big inspirational quotes from tech-founders.
- Pricing "Contact sales" without a self-serve tier.
- Heavy gradients on buttons (purple-to-pink reflex).
- Cute mascots.

## Example brands matching this register

- linear-app, vercel, cursor, raycast, framer, supabase, posthog, sentry, mintlify, resend, replicate

## Sample copywriting voice

- "The interface for AI."
- "Issue tracking you'll enjoy using."
- "Build. Ship. Scale."
- "Open the changelog →"

Clipped. Confident. Lower-case OK in body. Verbs lead. Numbers cited.

## Pairing

- Compatible directions: `tech-utility` (best), `modern-minimal`.
- Compatible archetypes: `dashboard`, `landing`, `pricing`, `chat`.
- Avoid pairing: `editorial-monocle` (mismatched tone), `soft-warm` (loses tech edge).

## Cultural anti-pattern

If your hero says "Reimagine X" or "The future of Y" — you've drifted into generic AI-startup-slop. Specific verbs only.
