# Angle Bank — Reusable Framing Lenses

These are framing angles that work on any build/system topic. Use instead of or layered on top of an arc from arc-types.md.

## The Mirror
**When:** After running a system long enough that it reveals something about you, not just organizes data.
**Core idea:** You built it to organize X. It ended up organizing you.
**Hook pattern:** "i built X to [stated purpose]. after [time], i realized it was [unexpected deeper purpose]."
**Emotion:** Surprise + self-awareness. Philosophical but grounded.

## The Missing Third Verb
**When:** Everyone talks about two steps but misses the third that makes the whole thing work.
**Core idea:** Write + read isn't enough. The third verb (promote / ship / delete / review) is what most people skip.
**Hook pattern:** "everyone builds [verb 1] and [verb 2]. nobody builds [verb 3]. that's why it breaks."
**Emotion:** "I didn't know I was missing this" — quiet aha.

## Personify the Automation
**When:** A background process (cron, hook, agent) does something that feels almost human.
**Core idea:** Give the automation a personality. Describe what it does as if it has intent.
**Hook pattern:** "every night at [time] something [does human-sounding thing]. it's not me. it's [what it actually is]."
**Emotion:** Curiosity + mild uncanniness. Makes technical things feel alive.

## Compound Interest
**When:** A system's value grows over time, not just linearly but exponentially.
**Core idea:** [X] doesn't compound. [Y] does. Here's what changes after [time].
**Hook pattern:** "[common thing] doesn't compound. [your thing] does."
**Emotion:** FOMO + aspiration. Makes reader want to start now.

## Obsolescence / TTL
**When:** Old information actively hurts — stale notes, outdated rules, wrong assumptions still surfacing.
**Core idea:** Knowledge needs an expiry date. Most systems only add, never prune.
**Hook pattern:** "my old [notes/rules/context] used to haunt me. built a [system] that gives knowledge a TTL."
**Emotion:** Recognition (everyone has stale notes) + relief that there's a fix.

## The Scope Creep Feature
**When:** What started as a small fix grew into a whole system — and that's a good thing.
**Core idea:** The rabbit hole is the point. Scope creep = the system finding its natural shape.
**Hook pattern:** "started with [small thing]. now i have [list of things]. it just keeps going."
**Emotion:** Mild vertigo + curiosity. Reader wonders how far their own rabbit hole goes.

## Anti-Organization
**When:** You replaced manual organizing with a system that does it for you — or stopped organizing entirely and things got better.
**Core idea:** You stopped doing X manually. The system improved. The contrarian insight: organizing is what you do when your system can't remember.
**Hook pattern:** "i stopped [manual effort]. [result that sounds wrong]. here's what actually happened."
**Emotion:** Contrarian recognition — everyone's exhausted by Notion/PKM theater. Immediate "wait, what?" reaction.
**Note:** Works for any system that replaces curation, filing, tagging, or sorting with automation.

## Brain Architecture
**When:** You have a technical system with multiple layers that happen to map to how brains work — hot recall, long-term storage, connections between concepts, pruning/cleanup.
**Core idea:** You didn't copy neuroscience. You accidentally re-derived it. Each layer appeared because the previous one broke without it.
**Hook pattern:** "my [system] has [N] layers. i didn't design them. my brain apparently did."
**Emotion:** Mild uncanniness + "oh that's why my brain does that" recognition.
**Note:** Hot memory = files loaded every session. Long-term = wiki/docs searched on demand. Synapses = graph/links between concepts. Sleep = nightly cleanup cron.

## Emergent Re-Derivation
**When:** You built something organically (problem → fix → fix for the fix) and ended up with a structure that matches a well-known architecture, design pattern, or natural system — without intending to.
**Core idea:** You didn't design it. You re-derived it. Every layer was inevitable given the previous one.
**Hook pattern:** "i wanted [small thing]. now i have [full system]. turned out [name of known pattern] was inevitable."
**Emotion:** Validation + mild vertigo. Builders recognize this feeling — scope creep that turned out to be the right shape.

## The Unexpected Teacher
**When:** The process of building revealed something you didn't set out to learn.
**Core idea:** You thought you were building [X]. You were actually learning [Y].
**Hook pattern:** "thought i was building [X]. turned out i was [what you were actually doing]."
**Emotion:** Humility + insight. Disarms the flex by making the builder look surprised.
