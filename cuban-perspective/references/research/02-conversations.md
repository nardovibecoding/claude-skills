## 一、GrowCo Conference演講（Inc. Magazine主辦）

**背景**：Inc. GrowCo大會，主持人對話形式。Cuban無提詞器，全程即興。演講開場警告：
[一手] "If anybody is upset by cursing, first of all, screw you. But you're going to be upset a lot. So I apologize in advance."

### 論創業起點：員工vs創業者

[一手] "I had a job out of college working for a bank for a short period of time, Mellon Bank. And I started a rookies club... my boss calls me in and starts screaming at me. How could you do this? You go everything through me. You work for me."

[一手] "And so those back-to-back experiences confirmed what I already knew, that I was a shitty-ass employee and I better start my own."

[推斷] Cuban的原點故事：他被兩個老闆依次開除，才成為創業者。這不是選擇，是被迫。和他日後強調的「只有被逼才能看清」主題一致。

### 論Broadcast.com起源

[一手] "A buddy of mine, Todd Wagner, came to me. There was no place to get audio or video online. So I bought a Packard Bell computer, set it up in the second bedroom of my house, got an ISDN line — broadband back then, 128K."

[一手] "We put it online. We called it AudioNet. And I just went everywhere I could and told people, you need to check this out."

[一手] "We were kind of the YouTube of the start of the Internet. And we just grew that and grew that, then did video and just dominated audio and video until we sold to Yahoo. $5.7 billion."

### 論Broadcast.com賣出後保住財富

[一手] "One, I wasn't greedy. I went on CNBC. They're like, don't you feel stupid? The stock is up another 30%. I'm like, yeah, I feel real stupid flying around in my G5."

[一手] "I had been in the technology business so long, I had seen the PC bubble come and burst. I had seen the local area network bubble come and burst. It was no shock that the internet bubble was going to burst."

[推斷] Cuban的反向操作：他在互聯網泡沫頂峰賣出並對沖，被媒體嘲笑，後來被稱為「華爾街十大最佳交易之一」。他對自己的判斷比對市場共識的信心更高。

### 論Dallas Mavericks收購

[一手] "I remember going to the Mavs game, the start of the 99-2000 season, and it wasn't a sellout. There wasn't a lot of energy in the arena. I'm like, damn, we're undefeated. This is the time to be excited."

[一手] "The first rule I installed was: if you mention our win-loss record, you're fired. Period. End of story. Because we don't sell wins or losses."

[一手] "We're not in the basketball business. We're in the business of creating experiences and memories."

[一手] "Having a great experience at a Mavs game is more like a good wedding. At a great wedding, Aunt Susie has her one glass of wine, is tipsy, dancing with cousin Johnny. Everybody's loving every minute of it and can't believe it."

### 論與NBA的衝突

[一手] "I get a call the next day from the league saying I was getting fined $100,000. I'm like, what? What did I do? They said because I sat on the floor next to the equipment people, it was conduct unbecoming an owner."

[一手] "And it's like, okay, I'm going to do it again. And if you don't like it, you'll keep on fining me, which they did until they stopped."

[一手] "[David Stern] was like, it's not me, it's the other owners who think that you're trying to show them up. And I'm like, this is how I've always run my businesses."

[二手] 被罰款總額超過$1.7-1.8 million（Cuban的自我更正：實際超過官方公布的$1.3M）

### 論管理風格

[一手] "When you first start working for me, directly for me, I micromanage until I trust you. So you're going to get back and forth with me daily, continuously."

[一手] "Once we get going and you start to understand how I think and what I expect, then you give me weekly reports."

[一手] "I always want bad news first. I don't want the 'oh, we had a great week.' I want to read, goddammit, we lost this sale and here's why. Because I expect you to do well. Every company is going to have setbacks. I want to know what those problems are so I can help you."

### 論競爭的本質

[一手] "Why do I do this? Because I love to compete. Somebody out there is competing against me, and I want to kick your ass."

[一手] "Business is the ultimate sport. An NBA game is 48 minutes. You practice a couple hours a day. In business, it's 24 by 7 by 365 by forever, and you compete against everybody."

### 論學生貸款危機（2014年觀點）

[一手] "Right now, we've seen college tuitions skyrocket. They've skyrocketed because of the easy money available to colleges."

[一手] "Mark Cuban is running the economy. I go and I say, Sally Mae, the maximum amount you're allowed to guarantee for any student in a year is $10,000. Period. End of story."

[一手] "Until you fix the student loan bubble, all this other stuff is shuffling deck chairs on the Titanic."

### 論慈善（匿名原則）

[一手] "Yeah, I mean, there's a lot of times when I'll find somebody in need and try to do something, but the quid pro quo is they're not allowed to say where it came from."

[一手] "I want to do it because I'm selfish and it makes me feel good. I want to do it because I think it's the right thing to do, but I never want to do it because, okay, here's what other people might think."

## 二、Shark Tank現場語錄

### 投資哲學

[一手] "I make investments in people as much as businesses." — 看人先於看生意

[二手] Cuban在Shark Tank以最嚴格的財務盡調著稱：追問利潤率、分銷渠道、現金流

### 具體案例引述

**Simple Sugars（最喜歡的投資之一）**：
[一手] "She's like, okay, here's what I'm doing, Mark. Leave me alone. If you have a problem, say something, but otherwise get out of here." — Cuban最欣賞這種創業者

**Tower Paddleboard**：
[一手] "All the other sharks were like, you're an idiot. And I'm like, no. I like it because you know the industry cold. You know how you're going to sell."

[推斷] Cuban的投資邏輯：不看外表表現，看核心競爭力清晰度。一個熔斷的pitch可以有好的生意。

### 撤資原則

[一手] "About 30% of the time, people fudge the numbers in one way or the other." — 說明他盡調嚴謹的原因

[一手] "They said on air it cost a dollar per unit to make. Then you do due diligence. Yeah, it's a dollar to make — if you sell a billion."

## 三、訪談中的一致性主題

[推斷] 跨越十年以上的訪談，Cuban反覆回到同一個核心主題：

1. **Pittsburgh起點** — 垃圾袋→學生貸款買下酒吧→六人睡地板→被開除兩次。這段履歷在每次訪談都出現，是他identity的錨點
2. **技術的早期識別** — "People thought I was an idiot" 模式：他看到別人看不到的東西，被嘲笑，然後被證明正確
3. **不貪婪的反常識** — 在最高點退出，被罵傻子，最終成贏家
4. **競爭作為存在目的** — 不是為錢，是為了「踢對方屁股」的原始驅動力
5. **直接溝通文化** — 無論對老闆、球隊、投資對象，都要求直接和真實，不要包裝

## 四、訪談風格觀察

[推斷] Cuban作為被採訪者的特徵：
- 接管節奏：他在訪談中主動設定pace，不讓主持人主導
- 用具體軼事替代抽象原則：不說"I believe in X"，說"This thing happened, and here's what I learned"
- 對爭議話題不迴避：主動挑起Sterling種族歧視案，自我披露偏見，然後講清立場
- 廣告時機：GrowCo演講中多次自然植入"CyberDust" app — 把演講變成產品推廣，毫無尷尬
